Introduction

For our Major Project, we are working on GARBAI an AI-driven garbage classification system designed to revolutionize waste management by enabling accurate and efficient waste segregation. Improper waste disposal leads to environmental pollution, inefficient recycling, and increased landfill waste. GARBAI addresses these challenges by utilizing advanced artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to automatically classify waste into categories such as biodegradable, recyclable, hazardous, and non-recyclable. The system can be integrated into municipal waste collection systems, recycling plants, and commercial establishments to streamline waste sorting, reduce human error, and promote sustainability. By enhancing waste segregation at the source, GARBAI contributes to a cleaner environment, supports recycling efforts, and aids municipalities and organizations in achieving their waste management goals. With its user-friendly interface and real-time classification capabilities, GARBAI is a smart and scalable solution for individuals, businesses, and local governments looking to improve waste management efficiency and contribute to a greener future.

Existing Methods

To make GARBAI different from all existing waste classification solutions, we analyzed highly reputed AI-based waste management applications like DeepWaste, Winnow, and Recycleye, which take waste from the automated recognition and sorting process or end with it. However, these were little or less than 'what sets GARBAI apart.'

  • DeepWaste: A mobile application primarily developed using AI algorithms for recycling waste items only by knowing about whether that item will be recyclable or non-recyclable. However, it is mainly targeted at individuals only thereby not sufficiently implemented at any level for municipalities or businesses.
  • Winnow: An AI-based food waste management system, tailor-made for the hospitality industry. As expected of such a system, it would, however, focus on how to reduce food waste in restaurants and hotels rather than offering an end-to-end garbage classification system on different types of wastes.
  • Recycleye: This is an AI-powered vision system to offer a facility in automating sorting waste at recycling plants. Still, it is for industrial waste management only, demanding high-cost infrastructure, thus not available for municipalities and small organizations.

Modules

Our Methodology integrates Web Development tools to optimize AI – Driven Garbage Classification for Sustainable Waste Management for Household use. The project focuses on identifying and labeling various trash items in images using methods like the ResNet-50 neural network. Our study utilized around 2,467 images that depicted six different types of waste. The use of deep learning techniques thereby equips the proposed system with the capability to effectively address garbage classification issues on the target database. The dataset used in this study consisted of 2467 images of six classes of wastes. The employment of deep learning methods thus empowers the proposed system to successfully tackle garbage classification problems on the target database. Findings of this study shall eventually assist in the construction of an automated garbage classification system that could work to better accounting efficiency for recycling purposes. We're developing a system that uses machine learning to automatically sort garbage, which will enhance recycling and waste management.

Our first step is to collect and prepare data by using a dataset containing around 2,467 images. These images represent six categories of waste: plastic, metal, paper, glass, cardboard, and trash types. The data is split into two sections: 70% for training and 30% for testing. Training is used to teach the model, and testing checks if the model performs well on new data, it hasn't encountered before. These categories are trained and tested using the RESNET-50 convolutional neural network model, which is well-known for its ability to extract detailed features from data. We use PyTorch to train ResNet- 50 model for better image classification while ensuring maximum accuracy through proper data augmentation techniques. Additionally, optimization is done with AdamW optimization, categorical cross-entropy loss, and batch normalization to ensure stability and effective learning. Learning rate scheduling is also put in place to fine-tune performance and guarantee effective convergence. The trained model would also be evaluated against several performance metrics to ascertain its efficacy in garbage classification. Accuracy is calculated as the measure of the percentage of correctly classified images, providing a general overview of the performance of the model. After the training is completed, the model is saved as resnet50_garbage_model.pth, which we will use in our Backend development. Using Flask API the frontend (index.html, classify.html, stats.html, learn.html, ourteam.html) and the backend (python) are connected. It combinedly gives our web application which consists of Classify where the waste can be uploaded from the dataset. After uploading the waste image, the category will be predicted and it will display whether the waste is recyclable or it should be sent to landfill for disposal.

Our Methodology reduces the manual labor and the inefficiencies due to manual error and the congested landfills where all the waste either plastic, glass, paper, etc. are deposited due to irresponsible behavior of man. Now using GARBAI it helps man in identifying which waste category is to be sent to landfill and which should be recycled, ensuring a greener and cleaner Tomorrow.
